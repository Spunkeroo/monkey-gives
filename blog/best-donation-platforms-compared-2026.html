Choosing the right donation platform can mean the difference between keeping 95% of every dollar donated or losing 8% or more to fees and processing charges. For nonprofits processing thousands or millions of dollars in donations annually, that difference compounds into significant amounts that could otherwise fund programs.

We tested and compared the most popular donation platforms available in 2026, evaluating each on fees, features, ease of use, donor experience, integration capabilities, and customer support. Whether you are a small grassroots organization or a large national nonprofit, this guide will help you find the platform that fits your needs and budget.

Donorbox

Donorbox is one of the most popular donation platforms for small to mid-size nonprofits, and for good reason. Their embeddable donation forms are clean, fast, and mobile-optimized. Setup takes less than 15 minutes, and they integrate with virtually every website builder including WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, and custom sites.

Key Features

  • Embeddable donation forms with customizable branding
  • Recurring donation support (monthly, quarterly, annually)
  • Peer-to-peer fundraising campaigns
  • Text-to-give functionality
  • Donation matching integration
  • CRM with donor management and communication tools
  • Integrations with Salesforce, Mailchimp, QuickBooks, and 30+ other tools
  • Multi-currency and multi-language support

Pricing

Donorbox charges a 1.75% platform fee on all transactions plus Stripe or PayPal payment processing fees (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). They offer a free tier with basic features and paid plans starting at $139/month for advanced features like CRM and peer-to-peer fundraising. ACH/bank transfer payments reduce processing fees to 0.8% with a $5 cap.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Easy setup, clean forms, strong integrations, reasonable fees, good customer support
  • Cons: Platform fee adds up at scale, advanced features require paid plans, limited event management

GoFundMe Charity (Classy)

GoFundMe acquired Classy in 2022, creating a powerful combination of the world's largest personal fundraising platform with enterprise-grade nonprofit tools. GoFundMe Charity (powered by Classy) is designed for registered 501(c)(3) organizations and provides a comprehensive fundraising suite.

Key Features

  • Campaign pages with customizable templates and storytelling tools
  • Peer-to-peer fundraising with team and individual pages
  • Event registration and ticketing
  • Recurring giving programs with flexible schedules
  • Classy Pay (built-in payment processing)
  • Salesforce integration and API access
  • Real-time analytics and reporting dashboards
  • GoFundMe's massive donor network for discovery

Pricing

GoFundMe Charity charges no platform fee for standard use. Payment processing is 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction via Classy Pay. Premium plans with advanced features, dedicated support, and custom integrations are available at custom pricing for larger organizations.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: No platform fee, powerful campaign tools, strong brand recognition, event management built in
  • Cons: Learning curve for full feature set, premium features require enterprise pricing, less suitable for very small orgs

Givebutter

Givebutter has quickly become a favorite among smaller nonprofits and community organizations for its modern interface and tip-based pricing model. Instead of charging nonprofits a platform fee, Givebutter asks donors to leave an optional tip at checkout. This model means the nonprofit receives 100% of the donation minus standard payment processing fees.

Key Features

  • Beautiful campaign pages with video and social media integration
  • Peer-to-peer and team fundraising
  • Event ticketing and auction tools
  • Text-to-give with custom keywords
  • Built-in CRM and email communications
  • Livestream fundraising integration (Twitch, YouTube, Facebook)
  • Free plan includes all features

Pricing

Givebutter's free plan includes all features with no platform fees. Revenue comes from optional donor tips (averaging around 12-15% of donations). Payment processing is 2.9% + $0.30 via Stripe. A paid plan at $99/month adds white-label branding and removes the tip suggestion.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: All features free, modern UI, great for social fundraising, livestream integration
  • Cons: Tip model may feel awkward to some donors, less enterprise features, newer company with less track record

Zeffy

Zeffy is the only donation platform that charges absolutely zero fees to nonprofits, including zero payment processing fees. Like Givebutter, they use a tip-based model where donors are asked to leave a voluntary contribution. But unlike other platforms, Zeffy also covers the payment processing costs, meaning 100% of every donation goes to the nonprofit.

Key Features

  • Zero platform fees and zero processing fees
  • Donation forms, peer-to-peer fundraising, event ticketing
  • Membership management
  • Automatic tax receipts
  • Donor management CRM
  • Online store for merchandise

Pricing

Completely free for nonprofits. Zero platform fees. Zero payment processing fees. Revenue comes entirely from optional donor tips. Zeffy is a certified B Corporation and is venture-backed with the explicit mission of keeping the platform free permanently.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Truly free, 100% of donations go to the nonprofit, easy setup
  • Cons: Fewer advanced features than paid platforms, limited integrations, tip model sustainability is unproven long-term, Canadian company (some US nonprofits prefer US-based platforms)

PayPal Giving Fund

PayPal Giving Fund is a unique platform that acts as a grant-making organization. Donors contribute to PayPal Giving Fund (which is itself a 501(c)(3)), and the fund then grants the money to the chosen nonprofit. The advantage is that PayPal covers all processing fees, so 100% of the donation reaches the nonprofit.

Key Features

  • Zero fees of any kind for enrolled nonprofits
  • Access to PayPal's 400+ million active accounts
  • Donations through PayPal checkout, Venmo, and eBay for Charity
  • Automatic disbursement to enrolled nonprofits
  • Donor receives tax receipt from PayPal Giving Fund

Pricing

Completely free. No platform fees, no processing fees. PayPal absorbs all costs as part of its corporate social responsibility program. Nonprofits must enroll and be verified to receive funds.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Truly zero fees, massive donor reach, Venmo integration, eBay for Charity
  • Cons: Limited customization, disbursement delays (funds are granted, not transferred directly), nonprofit does not control the donor relationship, limited reporting

Network for Good

Network for Good is an all-in-one fundraising and donor management platform designed specifically for small nonprofits. It combines donation processing, CRM, email marketing, and reporting in a single platform, eliminating the need for multiple tools and integrations.

Key Features

  • Integrated CRM with donor profiles, giving history, and communication logs
  • Email marketing with templates and automation
  • Fundraising pages and donation forms
  • Coaching and training resources for fundraising
  • Reporting and analytics dashboards
  • Event management

Pricing

Plans start at $99/month for organizations with fewer than 1,000 contacts. The $199/month plan supports up to 5,000 contacts with additional features. Enterprise pricing is available for larger organizations. Payment processing fees are included in the monthly subscription.

Bloomerang

Bloomerang differentiates itself with a laser focus on donor retention. Their platform is built around the insight that retaining existing donors is far more cost-effective than acquiring new ones. The average nonprofit retains only 45% of first-time donors, and Bloomerang's tools are designed to improve that number.

Key Features

  • Donor retention scoring and engagement tracking
  • Generosity scores that predict future giving
  • Integrated email and letter campaigns
  • Wealth screening and prospect research
  • Online donation forms with recurring giving
  • Volunteer management
  • QuickBooks integration

Pricing

Plans start at $125/month for up to 1,000 records. Pricing scales based on the number of donor records. All plans include unlimited users, email, and training. Payment processing is included.

Fundraise Up

Fundraise Up uses artificial intelligence to optimize the donation experience in real time. Their platform analyzes donor behavior and automatically adjusts suggested amounts, payment methods, and page layout to maximize conversion rates. Organizations using Fundraise Up report an average 2x increase in online revenue.

Key Features

  • AI-powered donation optimization (suggested amounts, payment methods)
  • Checkout-style donation experience with Apple Pay, Google Pay, and crypto
  • Upsell tools that convert one-time donors to recurring
  • A/B testing built into every element
  • Stock and crypto donation processing
  • DAF (Donor-Advised Fund) integration
  • Enterprise-grade security and compliance

Pricing

Custom pricing based on organization size and volume. They offer a free tier for small nonprofits processing under $50,000 annually. Processing fees are competitive with Stripe rates.

Crypto Donation Platforms

Cryptocurrency donations are the fastest-growing category of charitable giving, with donors benefiting from significant tax advantages. Several platforms specialize in crypto-to-charity transactions:

The Giving Block

The industry leader in crypto philanthropy, The Giving Block supports 100+ cryptocurrencies and processes donations for over 2,000 nonprofits. They handle automatic conversion to USD, tax receipts, and compliance. Zero fees for donors; nonprofits pay a processing fee.

Engiven

Specializing in faith-based and smaller nonprofits, Engiven supports 100+ cryptocurrencies with same-day settlement. Their platform is simpler than The Giving Block but effective for organizations just starting to accept crypto.

Endaoment

An on-chain Donor-Advised Fund built on Ethereum, Endaoment provides full blockchain transparency for crypto philanthropists. Community-governed and fully transparent, it represents the future of decentralized charitable giving.

Why Crypto Donations Matter for Nonprofits

Crypto donors tend to give larger amounts than traditional donors. The average crypto donation through The Giving Block is over $10,000, compared to the average online donation of $128. Accepting crypto opens access to a new, younger, and wealthier donor demographic.

How to Choose the Right Platform

The best platform depends on your organization's specific situation. Here is a decision framework:

Choose Zeffy or PayPal Giving Fund if:

  • You are a very small nonprofit with minimal budget
  • Keeping 100% of donations is your top priority
  • You do not need advanced CRM or marketing features

Choose Donorbox or Givebutter if:

  • You need embeddable forms for your existing website
  • You want peer-to-peer and event fundraising capabilities
  • You want a balance of features and affordability

Choose GoFundMe Charity if:

  • You run large campaigns, events, or peer-to-peer fundraisers
  • You want access to GoFundMe's massive donor network
  • You need enterprise-grade tools and Salesforce integration

Choose Network for Good or Bloomerang if:

  • You want an all-in-one platform (CRM + fundraising + email)
  • You are focused on donor retention and relationship management
  • You want to consolidate multiple tools into one

Choose Fundraise Up if:

  • You are a mid-size to large nonprofit optimizing online revenue
  • You want AI-powered conversion optimization
  • You accept stocks, crypto, and DAF donations

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best donation platform with the lowest fees?

Zeffy charges zero platform fees for nonprofits by asking donors for an optional tip. PayPal Giving Fund also charges no fees for enrolled nonprofits. For platforms with fees, Donorbox charges 1.75% platform fee plus payment processing, which is among the lowest in the industry.

What is the difference between GoFundMe and GoFundMe Charity?

GoFundMe is for personal fundraising campaigns where donations are not tax deductible. GoFundMe Charity (formerly Classy) is for registered 501(c)(3) nonprofits, providing tax-deductible receipts, recurring giving, and advanced fundraising tools. Personal campaigns on GoFundMe are gifts, not charitable donations.

Do donation platforms provide tax receipts?

Platforms designed for nonprofits like Donorbox, Classy, and Givebutter automatically generate tax-deductible receipts for donors. Personal fundraising platforms like GoFundMe do not provide tax receipts because donations to individuals are not tax deductible. Always verify that your platform handles receipts properly.

Can I accept recurring donations on these platforms?

Yes, most nonprofit-focused platforms support recurring donations. Donorbox, Classy, Givebutter, and Network for Good all offer monthly and annual recurring giving options. Recurring donors typically give 42% more annually than one-time donors, making this a critical feature for sustainable fundraising.

Which donation platform is best for small nonprofits?

Zeffy is ideal for very small nonprofits because it charges zero fees. Donorbox is excellent for small to mid-size organizations with its low 1.75% fee and easy setup. Givebutter is also popular with small nonprofits for its free tier and modern interface. Start with a free or low-cost option and upgrade as your organization grows.
